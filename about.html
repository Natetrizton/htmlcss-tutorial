Atomic absorption spectrometry (AAS) is a technique in which free gaseous atoms absorb electromagnetic radiation at a specific wavelength to produce a measurable signal. The absorption signal is proportional to the concentration of those free absorbing atoms in the optical path. Therefore, for AAS measurements the analyte must be first converted into gaseous atoms, usually by application of heat to a cell called atomizer.
